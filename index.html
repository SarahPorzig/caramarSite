Country Living.

Property

Caramar takes it's name from an estate in Geelong off Thornhill Road where I grew up. There was a large homestead there and it owned all the land thereabouts, which has since been demolished and divided up into houses. There is still a Carramar Road that runs close to the site. I named this house after my childhood memories and removed one ‘r’ to make Caramar.

Susan Porzig

The property consists of 8.3 acres, 5 of which are bush. The remaining land is fenced off into 4 small paddocks, fully irrigated and with 3 stables. The property was originally used for emus and is currently used as a horse property.

Nearby attractions include the Ralph Illidge Sanctuary, Laang speedway, Panmure Gun club and many horse riding trails.

Flora & Fauna

The property is set in a sheltered hollow and contains a large variety of Australian bird life including Rosellas, Kookaburras and Black Cockatoos. A large contingent of Wallabies and Koalas live in the bush on the property. As the property retains rainfall very well, it contains Leeches that are farmed for use in hospitals.

Horses

Horses that have been adjisted here in the past include ‘Kiabe Tantara’ from the Kiabe Pony Stud in New South Wales and ‘Rajah’ who was Christina McVilly's first pony club horse (she is a Victorian State representative in Show Jumping).

There are currently two horses kept on the property - a quarter horse ‘Cookie’ and an ex pacer from New Zealand – ‘Billy-Bob’.

History

Our property here in Naringal was built a few months after the 1983 Ash Wednesday fires. There was no house here but neighbouring properties were literally blown up by the fires. The roof of the property next door was blown 100 metres away and the Naringal East School burnt out and never reopened. The house was built with the aim of surviving future bush fires, consisting of one rectangular roof with no angles to gather debris, concrete slab and irrigation throughout the property including the roof.
